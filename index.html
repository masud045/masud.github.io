About

Research profile and academic focus

My academic work focuses on process modeling, optimization, and intensification for sustainable chemical processes. I develop large-scale optimization frameworks, surrogate models, and AI-integrated decision systems for complex industrial applications, with particular interests in microwave-assisted processing, membrane systems, nutrient recovery, process control, and sustainability analysis.

Research Experience

Data-driven modeling and surrogate-based superstructure optimization of microwave-assisted ammonia production

Developed and validated machine learning models using limited and augmented experimental data to predict ammonia concentration and reactor performance, and performed MINLP-based process design optimization for energy-efficient and sustainable ammonia synthesis.

Superstructure optimization for sustainable membrane separations with process-structure-property relationships

Modeled membrane-based nutrient recovery systems, designed optimal configurations, and created a GUI prototype for automated membrane route generation under product and process constraints.

Data-driven model predictive control for intensified chemical processes

Developed high-fidelity dynamic models and fault-prognostic MPC strategies for high-risk batch reactors and AI-driven thermal optimization in intensified chemical systems.
